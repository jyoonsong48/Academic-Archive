## Lab 1: Pipetting & Dilutions
<br>o	Accuracy & Repeatability: Compare the accuracy of various pipettes (1 ml automatic vs. 10 ml glass) by measuring the mass of 1 ml of distilled water five times. Calculate mean and standard deviation to monitor reproducibility.
<br>o	Dilutions: Prepare 100 ml volumes of tartrazine solutions at 7 mg/l, 10 mg/l, and 12 mg/l concentrations from a 1 g/l stock solution. Verify dilutions visually and using a spectrophotometer.
<br>o	Solution Preparation: Calculate and prepare 10 ml of a 10% (w/v) saline (NaCl) solution. Perform serial dilutions to create 1 ml saline solutions at 5%, 2.5%, 1.25%, and 1% concentrations.

## Lab 2: Introduction to Spectrophotometry
<br>o	Absorption Spectrum: Measure the absorbance of a standard dye solution over a wavelength range of 500–700 nm in 10 nm increments. Zero the instrument with a "blank" water cuvette at each wavelength. Identify the $\lambda_{max}$ (wavelength of maximum absorption).
<br>o	Calibration Curve: Prepare a duplicate dilution series (S1–S5) of standard dye (1–5 g/ml). Measure absorbance at $\lambda_{max}$ and plot a calibration curve (Absorbance vs. Concentration) with a line of best fit.
<br>o	Unknown Concentration: Measure the absorbance of unknown solution 'X' in duplicate. Use the calibration curve equation ($y = mx + c$) to determine its concentration.

## Lab 3: Glucose Determination (GOD-PAP Assay)
<br>o	Standards Preparation: Dilute 100 mM glucose stock x100 to prepare a 1 mM solution. Create standards (S0–S5) ranging from 0–1.0 mM in 1 ml volumes.
<br>o	Serum Dilution: Prepare x2, x5, and x20 dilutions of a serum sample with unknown glucose concentration.
<br>o	GOD-PAP Assay: Transfer 0.1 ml of each standard, diluted serum, positive control, and negative control (serum + water) to test tubes in duplicate. Add 0.9 ml GOD-PAP reagent to all except negative controls.
<br>o	Analysis: Incubate for 25 minutes at room temperature. Read absorbance at 500 nm. Use RStudio to produce a calibration curve and determine the serum glucose concentration.

## Lab 4: Lowry Protein Assay
<br>o	Standard Curve: Prepare a dilution series of Bovine Serum Albumin (BSA) (0–300 µg/ml) in 3 ml volumes.
<br>o	CFE Dilution: Prepare x10, x50, and x100 dilutions of a bacterial Cell Free Extract (CFE).
<br>o	Color Development: Transfer 1 ml of each standard and CFE dilution into duplicate test tubes. Add 5 ml alkaline copper solution, wait 10 minutes, then add 0.5 ml Folin-Ciocalteu reagent.
<br>o	Measurement: Incubate for 30 minutes. Read absorbance at 750 nm (or 700 nm). Determine CFE protein concentration by rearranging the trendline equation from the BSA calibration curve.

## Lab 5: Gel Filtration Chromatography
<br>o	Column Setup: Pour a slurry of Sephadex G-50 into a 30 x 1.0 cm column and allow it to settle.
<br>o	Separation: Apply 0.2 ml of a dye mixture containing Dextran Blue (2,000,000 Da), Cytochrome c (13,000 Da), and Nitrazine Yellow (547 Da) to the gel bed.
<br>o	Elution: Feed phosphate buffer (pH 6.0) through the column. Collect eluate in 2 ml fractions.
<br>o	Detection: Measure the absorbance of blue fractions at 630 nm, red at 410 nm, and yellow at 464 nm. Plot an elution profile to estimate void volume ($V_o$), elution volume ($V_e$), and total bed volume ($V_t$).
