## Lab 1: Microscopy & Haemocytometer 
<br>1.	**Microscope Setup**: Plug in, switch on the light, and adjust the level. Position the condenser at the highest point and fully open the iris.
<br>2.	**Focusing**: Start with the x10 objective. Place a slide with a marked "X" on the stage, raise the stage until it nearly touches the lens, then move it down using the coarse and fine focus knobs until sharp.
<br>3.	**Eyepiece Adjustment**: Adjust the separation distance to see a single circular field and use the focus rings on the eyepieces to account for differences between your eyes.
<br>4.	**Cheek Cell Observation**: Place a drop of methylene blue on a slide. Scrape inner cheek cells with a sterile swab, smear them into the dye, and apply a coverslip.
<br>5.	**Haemocytometer Demo**: Observe a staff demonstration on the correct (and fragile) use of the counting chamber for yeast cell density calculations.

## Lab 2: Yeast Genetic & Phenotypic Variation
<br>1.	**Cell Counting**: Mix 10 µl of yeast culture with 10 µl of trypan blue. Load the haemocytometer and count viable (non-blue) cells in the four outer and one central square.
<br>2.	**Serial Dilution**: Perform 10-fold dilutions by transferring 20 µl of culture into 180 µl of sterile water repeatedly until reaching ~1,000 cells/ml.
<br>3.	**Plating**: Split four YPD agar plates in half with a marker.
<br>4.	**Inoculation**: Vortex yeast tubes, then add 50 µl of specific strains (WE, WA, NA, or mre11Δ) to designated plate halves.
<br>5.	**Spreading**: Sterilize a spreader using ethanol and a Bunsen flame, then spread the liquid across the agar.
<br>6.	**Incubation**: Incubate plates upside down for 2 days at either 30°C or 40°C (for temperature stress) or on MMS-containing agar (for DNA damage stress).

## Lab 3: Yeast Colony Analysis
<br>1.	**Colony Counting**: Collect plates and use a tally counter to determine the number of colonies on each half.
<br>2.	**Data Analysis**: Record counts in lab books and provide data to staff to calculate the percentage decrease in cell viability under stress.

## Lab 4: Plasmid Isolation & Restriction Digest
<br>1.	**Alkaline Lysis**: Resuspend an E. coli pellet (pBR322) in Buffer P1. Add Buffer P2 (lysis), then Buffer N3 (neutralization) to precipitate chromosomal DNA and proteins.
<br>2.	**Purification**: Centrifuge the mixture and transfer the supernatant to a Qiagen column. Wash the bound DNA with Buffers PB and PE, then elute using sterile MilliQ water.
<br>3.	**Quantification**: Use a Nanodrop spectrophotometer to measure A260/A280 ratios and DNA concentration.
<br>4.	**Restriction Digest**: Mix 10 µl of isolated DNA with mastermixes containing enzymes (SalI, EcoRI, PvuII) and incubate at 37°C for 10 minutes.

## Lab 5: Blood Typing & Gel Electrophoresis
<br>1.	**Gel Casting**: Pour 35 ml of warm agarose (containing GelRed) into a tray with end plates and a comb; let set for 15 minutes.
<br>2.	**Loading & Running**: Add loading dye to DNA samples and marker. Load 30 µl per well and run the gel at 60mA in TBE buffer for 40–50 minutes.
<br>3.	**Visualisation**: Image the gel using a UV gel documentation system to estimate fragment sizes.
<br>4.	**Blood Typing**: Prick a finger with a lancet, dilute a drop of blood in phosphate buffer, and place it on a WHO tray. Add Anti-A, Anti-B, and Anti-Rh sera and observe for agglutination.
