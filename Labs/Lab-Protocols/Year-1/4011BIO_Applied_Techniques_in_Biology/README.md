## Lab 1: DNA Purification and PCR Amplification
<br>1.	DNA Isolation: Purify human DNA from saliva using a spin protocol involving protease treatment, lysis buffer (AL), ethanol precipitation, and spin column washing (AW1, AW2) before eluting in Buffer AE.
<br>2.	Quantification: Use a Nanodrop spectrophotometer to measure DNA concentration and purity (260/280nm ratio).
<br>3.	PCR Setup: Prepare PCR tubes for TPA and STX loci by adding specific master mixes and 5µl of extracted DNA, control DNA, or water.
<br>4.	Thermal Cycling: Run TPA samples for 30 cycles (annealing at 58°C) and STX samples for 35 cycles (annealing at 60°C).

## Lab 2: Gel Electrophoresis of PCR Products
<br>1.	Gel Preparation: Pour 1.5% molten agarose containing GelRed into a casting tray with a comb and allow it to set.
<br>2.	Sample Loading: Mix 20µl of PCR product with 10µl bromophenol blue loading buffer and load into the gel wells.
<br>3.	Electrophoresis: Run the gel at 70mA for approximately 30 minutes in TBE buffer.
<br>4.	Visualization: Capture the gel image using a gel documentation chamber under UV light to determine the size of DNA bands.

## Lab 3: Restriction Digest and Ligation
<br>1.	Restriction Digest: Incubate pUC19 and pBK-CMVMnSOD plasmids with EcoR1 and Xba1 enzymes at 37°C for 10 minutes, followed by heat inactivation at 80°C.
<br>2.	Digest Analysis: Run 20µl of the digested samples on a 1.0% agarose gel to confirm successful cutting.
<br>3.	Ligation: Combine 3µl digested pUC19, 9µl digested pBK-CMVMnSOD, T4 DNA ligase, and buffer. Incubate at room temperature for 30 minutes.

## Lab 4: Transformation of Competent E. coli
<br>1.	Preparation: Resuspend pelleted E. coli XL1-Blue cells in ice-cold TSS buffer to make them chemically competent.
<br>2.	Transformation: Add ligation products or control DNA to competent cells on ice, heat shock at 42°C for 2 minutes, and recover in L-broth at 37°C for 30 minutes.
<br>3.	Plating: Spread 100µl of the transformation mixtures onto L-agar plates containing ampicillin, X-gal, and IPTG.

## Lab 5: Results and Interpretation
<br>1.	Data Collection: Count blue and white colonies on the transformation plates, excluding small satellite colonies.
<br>2.	Analysis: Calculate the number of viable bacteria per ml and determine the percentage of recombinant (white) colonies.

## Lab 6: Plasmid Minipreps and Final Analysis
<br>1.	Plasmid Isolation: Extract DNA from blue (B) and white (W) cultures using alkaline lysis (Buffers P1, P2, N3) and silica-membrane spin columns.
<br>2.	Quantification: Measure the concentration of the recovered plasmid DNA using the Nanodrop.
<br>3.	Restriction Analysis: Digest the isolated plasmids with EcoR1 and Xba1 (37°C for 10 minutes) to verify the presence of the 600bp MnSOD insert.
