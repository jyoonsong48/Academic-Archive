## Lab 1: Cell Culture (LTK-)
<br>1.	**Observation**: Examine live cultures in a 25 $cm^2$ flask using an inverted microscope to assess confluency.
<br>2.	**Washing**: Pour off old medium, add 10 ml PBS, and incubate for 2 minutes to remove medium traces and calcium/magnesium ions.
<br>3.	**Detachment**: Add 0.5 ml Trypsin/EDTA, incubate at 37°C for 3–5 minutes, and tap the flask to release cells.
<br>4.	**Resuspension**: Add 5 ml growth medium and pipette up and down 10 times to break clumps.
<br>5.	**Counting**: Mix a sample with Trypan Blue (1:1 ratio) and use a haemocytometer to count viable (unstained) cells.
<br>6.	**Inoculation**: Calculate and transfer the required volume of stock suspension into two new flasks to reach densities of $4 \times 10^2$ and $4 \times 10^3$ cells/$cm^2$.

## Lab 2: Cell Toxicity (Setting up GPCR Experiment)
<br>1.	**Cell Release**: Follow the subculture procedure (Wash with PBS, Detach with Trypsin/EDTA) to create a cell suspension.
<br>2.	**Cell Counting**: Use the haemocytometer and Trypan Blue to determine the viable cell concentration (cells/ml).
<br>3.	**6-Well Plate Inoculation**: Calculate the volume needed to seed $1 \times 10^5$ cells per well.
<br>4.	**Drug Treatment**: Add 200 $\mu$l of either 1 $\mu$M Drug A1, 1 $\mu$M Drug B1, or PBS (control) to designated wells.
<br>5.	**Incubation**: Incubate the plate for 24 hours at 37°C.

## Lab 3: Staining & Enzyme Assay (Part A)
<br>1.	**Crystal Violet Staining**: Fix cells with 5% formaldehyde, then stain with 0.01% Crystal Violet to visualize growth differences in the 6-well plate.
<br>2.	**Enzyme Time Course**: Mix PMP substrate and buffer in a cuvette. Add APase enzyme and immediately measure absorbance at 552 nm every 30 seconds for 5 minutes.
<br>3.	**Control**: Perform the same steps without the enzyme to measure un-catalysed hydrolysis.

## Lab 4: Enzyme Kinetics (Enzyme Concentration)
<br>1.	**Preparation**: Set up four cuvettes with varying enzyme concentrations (0, 75, 150, and 300 $\mu$g/ml).
<br>2.	**Assay**: Add substrate and buffer, then the specific enzyme volume. Measure absorbance at 552 nm every 30 seconds for 5 minutes.
<br>3.	**Unknown Sample**: Perform a time course for an "unknown" enzyme sample and use the generated standard curve to interpolate its activity.

## Lab 5: Michaelis-Menten & Lineweaver-Burk Analysis
<br>1.	**Substrate Dilution**: Prepare eight different concentrations of PMP substrate (0.0 to 0.557 mM) using buffer.
<br>2.	**Rate Measurement**: For each concentration, add 0.5 ml enzyme to 3 ml substrate/buffer mix and record absorbance at 552 nm for 5 minutes.
<br>3.	**Data Analysis**: Use average rates ($v_0$) to create Michaelis-Menten and Lineweaver-Burk plots to calculate kinetic constants.

## Lab 6: Isolation of Mitochondria & SDH Assays
<br>1.	**Fractionation**: Homogenize chilled peeled peas in maceration medium. Use differential centrifugation (1,000 rpm, then 10,000 x g) to produce Suspensions A (debris), B (mitochondria), and C (supernatant).
<br>2.	**SDH Assay**: Add suspensions to a mix of Succinate and DCPIP (blue dye). Measure the time taken for decolourisation (blue to colourless).
<br>3.	**Inhibition**: Repeat the SDH assay with Malonate to demonstrate competitive inhibition.
<br>4.	**Cytochrome Oxidase**: Add Nadi reagent to suspensions. A blue colour change indicates active cytochrome oxidase in intact mitochondria.
