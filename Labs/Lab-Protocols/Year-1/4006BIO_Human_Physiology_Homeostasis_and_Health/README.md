## <br> Lab 1: Surface Electromyograms (EMGs) & Muscle Fatigue
<br> Setup:  Remove jewellery, Connect Bio Amp cables, Attach dry earth strap, mark bicep&tricep -> attach electrode 
<br> o	Part A (EMG): Record surface EMGs from biceps and triceps during moderate and maximal contractions. Examine the effect of increasing weight (0–4 kg) on muscle amplitude.
<br>o	Part B (Fatigue): Calibrate a grip force transducer and measure fatigue at sustained contractions of 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% maximal strength.
<br>o	Part C (Encouragement & Rest): Measure the effects of verbal encouragement and brief (~0.5 s) rest on sustained maximal grip force.
<br>o	Visual Feedback: Observe the difference in maintaining 50% contraction with eyes open versus eyes closed.

## <br>Lab 2: Nerve Conduction Velocity
<br>o	Setup: Attach recording electrodes over the abductor pollicis brevis muscle and an earth strap around the wrist.
<br>o	Stimulation: Apply a stimulating bar electrode over the median nerve at the wrist. Increase pulse current (mA) until a maximal evoked EMG response is achieved.
<br>o	Velocity Calculation: Repeat stimulation at the medial side of the biceps tendon (elbow). Calculate conduction velocity using the difference in latency between wrist and elbow responses.

## <br>Lab 3: Spirometry
<br>o	Dynamic Tests: Perform Forced Vital Capacity (FVC) maneuvers to measure Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) and FEV_1.
<br>o	Simulated Asthma: Mimic bronchial obstruction by covering the filter with duct tape and a 5 mm hole, then repeat FVC tests.
<br>o	Static Measurements: Measure tidal breathing and calculate lung capacities (IC, EC, VC).

## <br>Lab 4: Electrocardiogram (ECG)
<br>o	Resting ECG: Record a standard Lead I ECG (right wrist to left wrist) and finger pulse simultaneously for 60 seconds.
<br>o	Exercise ECG: Perform 2 minutes of vigorous exercise (e.g., star jumps) and immediately record another ECG to compare intervals (PR, QRS, ST, TP) with resting data.

## <br>Lab 5: Blood Pressure Measurement
<br>o	Auscultation: Estimate systolic pressure via palpation, then use a stethoscope to identify Korotkoff sounds for precise systolic and diastolic readings.
<br>o	Variations: Test the effects of arm position (heart level vs. above head) and cuff size (infant, child, adult, leg) on measured pressure.
<br>o	Leg Measurement: Measure arterial blood pressure at the thigh (popliteal artery) and calf (dorsalis pedis artery) while lying and standing.
