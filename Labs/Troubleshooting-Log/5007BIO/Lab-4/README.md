 ## Minimum Inhibitory Concentrations (MICs)
### Problem: Bacteria didn't grow -> no zone of inhibition

<br> Failed disc diffusion tests (Kirby-Bauer) usually result from improper technique (e.g. uneven bacterial lawn inoculation, incorrect agar depth (standard is 4mm), or improper incubation temperature/time) (Davis and Stout, 1971). Other causes could be: using expired antibiotic discs, improper storage of discs, or incorrect inoculum density.

<br> But in this case: bacteria did not grow at all! Possible reasons for this can be:
<br> 1. evaporation. If it occurs during the incubation of the agar, there may be a loss of water. This results in a decrease in colony size and inhibition of bacterial growth. (Bonnet et al., 2019)

However, in this experiment, most of the samples (including other people's) did not show bacterial growth. Therefore, It's mostly because of the temperature setting or use of culture with incoreect inoculum density.
