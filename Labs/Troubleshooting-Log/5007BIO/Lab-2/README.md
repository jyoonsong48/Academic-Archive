 ## Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) Based Identification of _Meticillin Resistant Staphylococcus Aureus_ (MRSA)
### Problem: unreadable gel electrephoresis data (blur)
![5007BIO_Lab2_pic](https://github.com/jyoonsong48/Academic-Archive/blob/main/Labs/Visual-Gallery/5007BIO/5007BIO_Lab2_pic.PNG)
#### Note. The original aim of the experiment was to define the clinical isolate by checking the presence of the mecA gene via DNA extraction, PCR, and gel electrophoresis. 

<br> The unexpected nanodrop spectra peak at 230nm from all three DNA extractions led to a significantly lower 260/230 ratio (0.10-0.102) than the ideal, which is 2.3-2.4. (Koetsier and Cantor, 2019) A possible reason for this is insufficient removal of buffer AW1 and AW2, which contain guanidine hydrochloride and ethanol. (QIAGEN, 2021) 

<br> As reported from The Jackson Laboratory (2019), a 25µL PCR reaction typically requires ~5ng of pure DNA or 40-50ng of lower-quality DNA. The low DNA purity can be the reason for the blundered original data, however, while it is true that there was significant contamination of the DNA extracted, the sample gel electrophoresis result showed clear bands, which indicates that the PCR was successfully conducted. A possible explanation for this is the inhibitor tolerance of the PCR mastermix. According to Hedman et al. (2011), different sources of DNA polymerase can make it differ in its tolerance therefore, an appropriate DNA polymerase-buffer system reduces PCR inhibition, which can be an alternative to DNA purification. 

<br> As the PCR was successful, it can be inferred that the original data was unreadable due to failed gel electrophoresis. The reasons can be overloaded samples, physical agitation during running. To review the errors and improve the results, the experiment can be conducted in duplicates while applying the methods to enhance the purity of DNA.
