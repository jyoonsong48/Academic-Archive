 ## Blood DNA Extraction
### Problem: Low purity (low yield)

According to MPBio, possible reasons for the low yield of extracted DNA include incomplete blood cell lysis, protein precipitates clogging the membrane, and an insufficient number of blood cells in the starting sample. For optimal blood cell lysis, one could increase the incubation time with the lysis buffer, adjust the speed or time of agitation, or use a more aggressive lysing matrix. <br>
Clogging of the membrane from protein precipitates typically results from using species with high haemoglobin concentrations, such as horses or guinea pigs (Esa et al., 2018). Lastly, an insufficient number of blood cells can result from an inadequate amount of the sample itself or anemia in the sample. Therefore, it is inferred that the problem was resulted from incomplete blood cell lysis or insufficient blood cells.

To solve this problem, collecting adequete amount of samples, increasing incubation time with the lysis buffer, or using appropritate lysing matrix can be employed to the experiment.
